Lebanon has a long and rich history, shaped by many cultures and civilizations over time.

Anjar

Built in the 8th century by the Umayyad Caliphate, Anjar is a remarkable archaeological site in the Bekaa Valley. Its symmetrical design, colonnades, and palaces reflect the grandeur of early Islamic urban planning.

Baalbek

Constructed by the Romans in the 1st century, Baalbek is home to the colossal Temples of Jupiter and Bacchus. These awe-inspiring ruins showcase Roman engineering at its peak and remain one of the largest ancient temple complexes in the world.

Beit El Dine

Completed in 1812 by Emir Bashir Shihab II, Beiteddine Palace is a masterpiece of Ottoman-inspired Lebanese architecture. Its intricate mosaics, lavish courtyards, and mountain views make it a cultural treasure.

Faqra

Dating back to the Roman era, the Faqra Ruins in Kfardebian include temples, altars, and tombs carved into limestone. This site reflects a blend of Roman and Phoenician influences amid a stunning mountain setting.

Sidon Castle

Built by the Crusaders in 1228, Sidon’s Sea Castle is a fortress overlooking the Mediterranean. It served as a strategic stronghold and offers breathtaking views of Sidon’s ancient harbor.

Tripoli Castle

Also known as the Citadel of Raymond de Saint-Gilles, Tripoli Castle was built in 1103 by the Crusaders. This massive fortress sits atop a hill, offering panoramic views of Tripoli. Expanded during the Mamluk and Ottoman periods, it is a testament to the city’s strategic importance throughout history.
